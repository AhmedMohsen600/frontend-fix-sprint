# Money Strategy

## Chosen path

Use Option 5: a productized frontend bug-fixing and UI improvement service.

## Why this is the fastest path

This path sells Ahmed's existing skill directly. It does not require a large audience, template marketplace approval, paid ads, product validation, backend infrastructure, or ongoing SaaS support.

The target is simple:

- 2 projects/month at 250 USD each, or
- 1 monthly frontend maintenance retainer at 500 USD/month.

## Target buyers

- Bootstrapped SaaS founders with a messy React/Next.js frontend.
- Agencies that need overflow frontend help.
- Recruiters or hiring managers who need a senior contractor for small frontend tasks.
- Product teams with dashboards, landing pages, admin panels, or UI bugs they keep postponing.

## Positioning

Ahmed is not selling "frontend development" as a vague service. The offer is a fixed-scope sprint:

> I fix React/Next.js UI bugs, responsiveness problems, frontend polish issues, and small performance problems in a clean 3-day sprint.

## Revenue math

- Minimum monthly target: 500 USD.
- Entry offer: 250 USD/project.
- Monthly offer: 500 USD/month.
- Required volume: two small projects per month or one retained client.

## Fastest outreach angle

Do not pitch generic development. Pitch one visible pain:

- Broken mobile layout.
- Messy SaaS dashboard.
- Slow landing page.
- Figma page that needs implementation.
- UI bugs that block launch.
- Frontend backlog that needs quick cleanup.
